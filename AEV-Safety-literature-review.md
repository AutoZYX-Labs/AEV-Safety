# AEV Safety Literature Review

## Scope

This review consolidates the research evidence already collected for the AEV Safety manuscript. It focuses on autonomous engineering vehicles, mobile working-machine systems, autonomous excavation, wheel-loader automation, construction robotics, mining autonomy, space-resource operations, safety analysis and adjacent standards.

## Methodological Donor Domains

Road-vehicle safety methods provide problem structures:

1. ISO 26262 functional safety lifecycle.
2. SOTIF, Safety of the Intended Functionality, for intended-function insufficiencies and trigger conditions.
3. ISO/SAE 21434 cybersecurity engineering.
4. AI safety and learning-enabled component assurance.
5. Scenario-based testing and evidence coverage.
6. Safety cases as claim, argument, evidence and assumption structures.

These methods are useful as donors. Their labels and consequence models must be rewritten before being used for engineering vehicles.

## Engineering-Vehicle Research Themes

### Wheel Loader Automation

Key themes:

- Material-pile perception.
- Digging point selection.
- Digging strategy for different material types.
- Bucket fill-rate estimation.
- Autonomous unloading and dump-truck pose recognition.
- Path planning and tracking for articulated loaders.
- Repeated loading and unloading cycles.

Li Xuefei's business-plan material is important internal background. It frames loader automation as work-oriented autonomy rather than driving-only autonomy. It also identifies autonomous digging, unloading, fill-rate measurement and path tracking as practical engineering bottlenecks. These points support the MWMS, WTDC and DMWT framing, but should be treated as internal background unless a public or authorized source is cited.

### Excavation and Trenching

Key themes:

- Digital-plan alignment.
- Soil and material uncertainty.
- Bucket-pose uncertainty.
- Underground utility protection.
- Trench-depth and slope quality.
- Recovery after interruption.

Autonomous excavation research supports technical feasibility, but it does not by itself close a safety case for pipeline trenching, utility protection or construction-quality acceptance.

### Construction Robotics and Solar Piling

Key themes:

- Repeatable task automation.
- Pile position, verticality and depth.
- RTK/GNSS and localization degradation.
- Exclusion zones and remote stop.
- As-built data logging.

Public product pages can anchor scenarios, but they cannot prove customer-side safety maturity or acceptance results.

### Autonomous Mining and Work Systems

Autonomous haulage and open-pit mine studies show that autonomy redistributes human work instead of removing it. Control-room operation, on-site organization, communication, training and continuous adaptation are part of the safety argument.

### Space Resources and Extreme Environments

The Move the Earth and Beyond material connects construction/mining automation with lunar and Mars resource utilization. NASA, DLR and related space-resource references support the strategic extension: future planetary mining is still a mobile working-machine safety problem, with harsher communication, recovery, environment and maintainability constraints.

## Evidence Boundary

This review separates:

1. Public peer-reviewed or official evidence.
2. Public product or company pages.
3. Internal collaborator background material.
4. User-reported discussion notes.
5. Evidence that still requires authorization or field confirmation.

Only the first two categories can be directly cited in public text, and even public product pages cannot be used to infer certification, operating KPI or customer acceptance.
