# Contributing

[中文](CONTRIBUTING.md)

AEV Safety welcomes contributions from researchers, engineers, standardization experts, construction/mining/port/solar-site operators and testing organizations.

## Contribution Areas

1. Literature and standards leads

   Prefer DOI pages, publisher pages, official standards pages, regulator pages and public company pages. Do not use unverified secondary excerpts as factual evidence.

2. Scenarios and task cases

   Contributions may cover loading, unloading, digging, trenching, piling, hauling, hold cleaning, material transfer, charging, fault recovery and remote supervision. Please describe machine type, worksite, material state, task boundary, exposed people/assets, potential consequences and evidence source.

3. Standards mapping

   Useful mappings include ISO 17757, ISO 19014, ISO 15817, ISO 21815, ISO 21448, ISO 26262, ISO 34502, ISO/PAS 8800, ISO 18497-1, robot SOTIF projects and Chinese construction/mining machinery standards.

4. Engineering practice

   Useful practice inputs include anonymized field experience, test-record fields, operational-monitoring metrics, recovery procedures, remote-supervision procedures and safety-case structures. Company and customer information must be anonymized before submission.

## Evidence Boundaries

- Public papers and authorized standards can support research conclusions and clause-level mapping.
- Standards project notices, catalogue pages and official status pages can support status and scope only; they do not replace the standard text.
- Public company pages, press releases and videos can anchor scenarios; they do not prove product maturity, certification status or long-term safety performance.
- Internal business plans, project decks and meeting discussions are background inputs unless independently verified.

## Terminology

- Use SOTIF, Safety of the Intended Functionality and 预期功能安全.
- Do not use the common incorrect literal rendering as the English name of ISO 21448 or SOTIF.
- Define MWMS, WTDC and DMWT with their full Chinese and English names at first use.

## Submission Style

Please submit by module:

- `taxonomy/`: scenario and task taxonomy.
- `architecture/`: reference architecture, safety monitoring and evidence flow.
- `benchmarks/`: evaluation metrics and testing matrix.
- `standards/`: standards landscape and mapping.
- `paper/`: manuscript and references.
- `docs/`: website, reviews and source synthesis.

Each substantive update should maintain both Chinese and English versions.
